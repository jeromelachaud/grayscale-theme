Inattention Blindness

Inattention blindness is a concept that has been used to explain the phenomenon whereby an observer fails to notice an appearing stimulus when focusing attention on a present stimulus. Knowing a distraction will appear can cause the observer to force his or her attention more strongly on the primary stimulus and inhibit the attraction of the distracter. When the observer is already attuned to focusing on a particular set of visual characteristics, he or she may be more likely to notice a distracter that shares these characteristics.

Conclusion

This study found that banner blindness is strongest when users are goal directed and the banner advertisement is located in an area where users can reliably anticipate advertising will appear. The shape of the visual field during situations of banner blindness seems to be more vertically aligned than horizontal in nature.

Take-aways

Users can predict the locations of advertising and effectively block out those sections. Animated may be one way to capture and hold users attention. Keep advertising non-obtrusive, as users may have a negative reaction to those that are.
